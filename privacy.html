Privacy Policy

Effective date: 28 May 2026 · App: codes. — Vouchers & Promo Manager · Publisher: EVDiag

Short version: codes. stores everything on your device. We do not track you or run any analytics. There are two external services, used only if you choose the relevant features: RevenueCat processes one-time in-app purchases, and our optional cloud sync (part of the Pro Online tier) relays end-to-end-encrypted messages between your team's devices. Even with cloud sync, our server never sees the contents of anything you store — every payload is encrypted on your device before it leaves it.

1. Who we are

codes. is an application published by EVDiag. For privacy questions, contact us at service@evdiag.net.

2. What data the app collects

On-device data: The app stores your promo codes, voucher templates, customer contact entries, team configuration, and category settings locally on your device. By default this data never leaves your device.

No analytics or telemetry: We do not embed any analytics SDK, crash reporting service, or telemetry collector in the application. We do not know how often you open the app, what features you use, or what data you create.

Account / identifier — only if you enable cloud sync: The app does not require you to create an account, give an email, or log in. If — and only if — you turn on online sync (an opt-in Pro Online feature, off by default), the app generates a random, anonymous device identifier (for example u_3f9a…) and an authentication token, and registers them with our cloud-sync service so your devices can exchange messages. This identifier is not linked to your name, email, or any real-world identity. See Section 5.

3. Permissions the app requests

The app may request these device permissions, all used exclusively for the stated purpose:

  • Camera: to scan QR codes for voucher redemption and team setup. Scans are processed entirely on your device.
  • Notifications: to schedule local reminders (e.g. daily team status reports). Notifications originate from your device, not from any remote server.
  • Photo library / storage: to save voucher images and PDF exports you choose to create. We do not read existing files except those you specifically open with codes..
  • Internet: used for in-app purchase processing (see Section 4), optional cloud sync (see Section 5), and for opening external links you explicitly tap (such as this privacy policy).
  • Bluetooth (Android only): reserved for future thermal-printer support. Currently unused. Declared with the neverForLocation flag — never used to determine location.

4. Third-party services

RevenueCat (privacy.revenuecat.com) processes in-app purchases. When you make a purchase, RevenueCat receives:

  • A randomly-generated anonymous identifier for your device
  • The product purchased (one of: pro_team_unlock, pro_online_unlock, pro_online_upgrade)
  • The receipt from Apple App Store or Google Play, used to verify the purchase is genuine
  • Standard transaction metadata (country, price, currency, purchase date)

RevenueCat does not receive any of your code, voucher, customer, or team data. Its role is limited to verifying purchase receipts with Apple/Google and informing the app whether you have an active Pro or Pro Online entitlement.

Cloudflare (cloudflare.com/privacypolicy) hosts our optional cloud-sync server (see Section 5). As the network and database provider, Cloudflare processes the encrypted relay traffic and, like any web host, its edge logs may record connection metadata such as IP address, request URL, and timestamp under Cloudflare's own retention policy. Cloudflare cannot read message contents, which are end-to-end encrypted before transmission.

Apple App Store / Google Play: when you install or update the app, or make an in-app purchase, your interaction with these stores is governed by Apple's and Google's respective privacy policies. We do not control what data they collect.

5. Team sharing — offline files and optional online sync

Team mode lets two devices share a code catalog. Transfers can happen two ways, and in both cases the payload is encrypted on your device with AES-GCM using a shared team key that you create and that never leaves your devices — we never have it.

Offline (default): peer-to-peer files. You exchange encrypted .pcm files (codes. package format) through whatever channel you choose (AirDrop, WhatsApp, email, file-sharing apps). No server controlled by us is involved at any point. If you use a third-party channel like WhatsApp or email, that channel's privacy policy applies to the file in transit. Once received and opened in codes., the payload is decrypted on-device and processed locally.

Online (optional, Pro Online): encrypted relay. If you enable online sync, the same encrypted messages are routed through our cloud-sync server instead of shared manually. The server acts as a "dumb mailbox":

  • What the server can read: nothing of substance. Message contents (your codes, vouchers, names, and team data) are end-to-end encrypted; the server only ever stores an opaque encrypted blob.
  • What the server can see (metadata): the sender and recipient anonymous device identifiers, the size of each encrypted message, and the time it was sent or received. It cannot tell who anyone is by name.
  • What we deliberately do not log: we do not log message contents, recipients, or senders in our application logs. (Cloudflare's edge logs capture connection metadata as described in Section 4.)

6. Data retention

On your device: your local data persists as long as codes. is installed. Uninstalling the app deletes all locally-stored data (with the backup caveats in Section 9).

Cloud sync (if used): relayed messages are deleted from the server as soon as the recipient device confirms receipt, or automatically after they expire — a default of 7 days, never more than 30. An hourly cleanup removes anything past its expiry. Anonymous sync accounts that have been inactive for 90 days and hold no pending messages are automatically deleted.

RevenueCat: purchase records are retained per RevenueCat's policy. You may request deletion of your RevenueCat anonymous identifier by contacting us at service@evdiag.net.

7. Deleting your cloud-sync data

If you have used online sync, you can remove your server-side data at any time:

  • In the app: Settings → online sync → "Delete sync account". This wipes your inbox and your anonymous user record from our server. Messages you already sent to a teammate remain in their inbox until their device receives them or they expire (they are that teammate's data to manage), and they are end-to-end encrypted throughout.
  • By email: contact service@evdiag.net and we will delete your sync data.
  • Automatically: all relayed messages expire (see Section 6), and inactive accounts are reaped after 90 days, so abandoned data does not linger.

If you never enable online sync, there is no server-side data to delete.

8. Children's privacy

codes. is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child has interacted with the app and you wish to report a concern, contact service@evdiag.net.

9. Your rights (GDPR, CCPA, and similar laws)

For data held on your device, you are in full control — it is yours, on your hardware.

For the limited data our cloud-sync server holds when you enable online sync (an anonymous device identifier plus end-to-end-encrypted messages and their metadata), and for data held by RevenueCat (an anonymous identifier and purchase history), you can:

  • Request a copy of the data we or RevenueCat hold about you
  • Request deletion (see Section 7 for the in-app and email options)
  • Withdraw consent by turning off online sync and/or ceasing to use the app

Because all of this data is anonymous (no name, email, or account profile), it cannot be tied back to your identity by us. Contact service@evdiag.net to exercise these rights.

10. Device backups

If you have enabled iCloud Backup (iOS) or Google Drive Backup (Android), your device's operating system may include codes.'s local data in those backups. Those backups are encrypted by Apple or Google respectively, and we have no access to them. Refer to Apple's and Google's documentation for details on managing your backups.

11. Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we will update the effective date at the top and post the revised policy at this URL. Material changes will be highlighted in the app's release notes.

12. Contact

For any privacy-related question: service@evdiag.net.
